# Operational Risk Management Policy
### Sample / synthetic — for testing the Phase 3 gap-analysis chain only

> This is a fictitious policy created to exercise the multi-agent mapper and gap agents. It deliberately contains coverage gaps. Do not treat it as a real or complete policy.

## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy sets out the framework for identifying, assessing, monitoring and controlling operational risk across the Bank. It applies to all business lines, support functions and branches. Operational risk is defined as the risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people and systems, or from external events. This definition includes legal risk but excludes strategic and reputational risk.

## 2. Governance and the Three Lines

The Bank operates a Three Lines model. The first line owns and manages risk within its activities. The second line, Operational Risk Management, sets the framework, provides challenge and oversight, and reports independently. The third line, Internal Audit, provides assurance over the design and operating effectiveness of the framework.

The Operational Risk Committee meets quarterly and reviews the risk profile, material incidents, and the status of remediation actions. Material matters are escalated to the Board Risk Committee.

## 3. Risk taxonomy

Operational risk is categorised into: internal fraud, external fraud, employment practices, clients and products, damage to physical assets, business disruption and system failures, and execution and process management. Each category has a designated second-line owner.

## 4. Risk and Control Self-Assessment

Each business line completes an RCSA at least annually. The assessment identifies inherent risks, evaluates the design and effectiveness of controls, and determines residual risk against the Bank's risk appetite. Control owners are named and assessment results are reviewed by the second line.

## 5. Incident and loss data management

All operational risk incidents above the recording threshold must be logged in the incident register within ten business days of identification. Each incident records the gross loss, recoveries, root cause, and remediation actions. The second line reviews the register monthly and analyses themes.

## 6. Key Risk Indicators

Each business line maintains a set of Key Risk Indicators with defined thresholds and escalation triggers. Breaches are reported to the Operational Risk Committee. Indicators are reviewed annually for continued relevance.

## 7. Outsourcing

Material outsourcing arrangements require a risk assessment before engagement and ongoing monitoring of service levels. The business line retains accountability for outsourced activities. A register of material outsourcing arrangements is maintained by Procurement.

## 8. Business continuity

The Bank maintains business continuity plans for critical functions. Plans are tested annually. Recovery time objectives are defined for critical systems.

## 9. Policy review

This policy is reviewed at least every two years, or sooner following a material change in the regulatory environment or risk profile.
